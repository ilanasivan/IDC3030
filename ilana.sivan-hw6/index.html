I was born in Israel, but I grew up in Salt Lake City, Utah, and Boston, Massachusetts. Today I call Tel Aviv home. I'm a former researcher turned high-tech analyst and developer, but sometimes I think about being a chef.

I currently work as a Data Analyst at Aiola, a young start up located in Herzliya, Israel. I love data and numbers, problem-solving, and driving decisions with insights and creativity, but beyond the technical scope of my position I am also involved in scoping, client-side project leadership, and product development.

In my past-life and in my dreams, I'm one and the same, a researcher. I previously held appointments and completed projects in academic and industry laboratories. I'm particularly interested in the intersection drug development, immunology, cancer and bioinformatics. I was lucky enough to work on the team that released Keytruda and to have early access to working with STING. Aside from that, I mostly pipetted up and down and waited for my cell cultures.

When I'm not working, I study Computer Science at IDC Herzliya, and I look forward to continuing to a doctoral degree program sometime in the future. All this makes me pretty busy, but I still find time to enjoy the little things. My hobbies include cooking (and eating), dancing, reading, and I love riding my motorcycle (especially by the sea), and when I find time, I enjoy camping with loved ones and friends.
